## Short Bio (200 words)

Brooks Duvall is the founder of Hardlife Apparel Company (HRDLF), an independent streetwear brand he started in Philadelphia in 2006. He has run the brand for 19 years with zero outside investors, no employees, and no corporate backing — a federal trademark holder since day one.

Duvall grew up skating Venice Beach alongside Jay Adams and the original Dogtown crew. That skate culture DNA — Old English typography, skull-and-laurel crest, limited drops that never restock — has defined HRDLF for nearly two decades.

In 2025, he rebuilt the entire brand infrastructure using AI tools (Claude Code, ChatGPT, Grok) with no team and no budget. The case study is one of the first documented examples of a solo founder using AI to operate an entire fashion brand end-to-end.

In 2026, he launched HRDLFcoin, a Solana-based community token — making HRDLF the first established streetwear brand with a blockchain membership layer.

Duvall is available to comment on: independent brand building, streetwear culture, AI in fashion and small business, skate culture history, Web3 and community tokens, founder economics, and the realities of running a one-person brand.
