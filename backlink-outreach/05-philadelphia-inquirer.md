**Subject:** Local streetwear brand celebrates 20 years of independence

Dear Inquirer Business/Features desk,

I'm writing to share a story pitch about Hardlife Apparel Company — a Philadelphia streetwear brand I founded in 2006 that just reached its 20th year of operation.

In an era where most independent fashion brands last 2-3 years, HRDLF has survived two decades with zero outside investment, zero corporate partnerships, and a business model built on limited-run production that never restocks. Every piece is printed once and sold until it's gone — then the page comes down. That policy hasn't changed since day one.

The brand grew out of Philadelphia's skate scene and has maintained its roots in skate culture and street art throughout. This year, we took an unconventional step by launching HRDLFcoin on the Solana blockchain — a cryptocurrency that gives the first 100 holders permanent founding member status.

Our next collection, Graffiti Beach (a tribute to 1980s Venice Beach graffiti culture), drops April 2nd.

I'd be happy to sit down for an interview, provide high-resolution product photography, or connect over email. This is a Philadelphia story about survival, independence, and building something that lasts.
