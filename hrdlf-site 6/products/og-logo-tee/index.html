The Story

The Tee That Built
A Movement.

Philadelphia streetwear heritage since 2006

The OG Logo Tee is where Hardlife Apparel Company started. Before the hoodies, before the caps, before the lookbooks and the editorial shoots—there was a black tee with Old English lettering across the chest. Two words. No apology. HardLife.

This isn’t a throwback or a reissue. It’s the original design that launched a brand in 2006 and has been in continuous production ever since. The Old English typeface has become the visual signature of HRDLF—a nod to the blackletter tradition that runs through skateboarding, graffiti, and street culture from Los Angeles to Philadelphia and every city in between.

Why AS Colour heavyweight cotton matters

Most streetwear brands cut corners on blanks. We don’t. The OG Logo Tee is printed on AS Colour—a premium heavyweight cotton blank trusted by independent brands worldwide for its superior hand-feel, structural fit, and wash-after-wash durability. This is a tee that holds its shape and gets better with age, not one that pills and shrinks after the first cycle.

The screen print is applied by hand using high-density ink for a raised texture you can feel. Every detail—from the ribbed crewneck to the HRDLF neck label with the skull and laurel crest—reflects nearly two decades of refinement from an independent brand that answers to its community, not investors.

Old English typography as a streetwear staple

Old English blackletter fonts have been a cornerstone of streetwear since the culture’s earliest days. From LA Chicano lettering to East Coast skateboard graphics, the blackletter aesthetic carries weight, heritage, and attitude that no clean sans-serif can match. The HRDLF wordmark takes that tradition and makes it permanent—not as a trend, but as identity.

How do I care for my OG Logo Tee?
