## 1. BRAND OVERVIEW

**Legal name:** Hardlife Apparel Company LTD
**Brand name:** HRDLF (pronounced "Hard Life")
**Founded:** 2006, Philadelphia, PA
**Status:** Independent since day one. 19 years. No investors. No board. No outside capital.
**Founder:** Brooks Duvall
**Brand motto:** *Nothing awesome comes easy.*
**Current HQ:** Operating from Southern Norway (founder relocated). Brand DNA remains Philadelphia / Venice Beach.

### Founder Bio — Brooks Duvall
Brooks grew up on the edges of the scenes that built modern street culture. Venice Beach in the 1980s, when Jay Adams and the Dogtown crew were turning empty pools into a global movement. Graffiti on every wall. Skateboarding and street art at the absolute forefront of culture. That was his apprenticeship — not a classroom, not a corporate track.

He launched HRDLF in Philadelphia in 2006 as a direct response to the corporate takeover of streetwear. Every brand he respected was getting bought, diluted, or co-signed into irrelevance. He decided to build one that refused to play that game. 19 years later, HRDLF is still doing it the exact same way — independent, direct-to-fan, built slow, built right.

### Mission
To build the longest-running independent streetwear brand in America by refusing every shortcut that has killed the brands we came up respecting. No investors. No wholesale partners diluting the message. No restocks. No compromises on the product. Every drop is a limited run — when it's gone, the page comes down.

### Core Tenets
1. **Independence is the product.** The clothes are the artifact. The independence is the thing people are actually buying into.
2. **Limited runs only.** We don't restock. Not for any piece. Not ever.
3. **The archive is permanent.** Every drop lives forever as a record. The first 100 HRDLFcoin holders are archived the same way.
4. **Direct to the people who show up.** Subscribers get early access. Holders get earlier access. Walk-ons come last.
5. **No corporate language.** Brooks talks to customers the way he'd talk to a friend at a skate spot. Nothing else is allowed.

## 2. BRAND VOICE

### Who Is Writing
Every word that leaves HRDLF is written as Brooks — 60 years old, been in this game since the '80s, no patience for marketing-speak. He's not a kid trying to sound cool. He's a founder who has earned the right to be direct.

### Voice Rules
- **Short declarative sentences.** No padding. No throat-clearing.
- **Start with the point.** Lead with the statement, not the setup.
- **First person when it matters.** "I made this" > "We crafted this."
- **"Let me be straight with you" is allowed.** That's the voice. Use it when it fits.
- **Signoff:** `— Brooks` for founder-voice pieces. `— HRDLF` for brand-voice pieces.
- **No hype words:** NEVER use "amazing," "revolutionary," "game-changing," "unleash," "elevate," "curated," "exclusive" (unless strictly literal), "premium" (unless strictly factual), "unlock."
- **No hedging:** NEVER "we think," "hopefully," "we're excited to announce."
- **Cultural anchors allowed:** Venice Beach 1985, Philadelphia 2006, Jay Adams, Dogtown, graffiti, skate culture, street culture, punk-era independence.
- **The 3 lines we reuse:**
  1. *Nothing awesome comes easy.*
  2. *19 years. No investors. No shortcuts.*
  3. *When it's gone, the page comes down.*

### What the Voice Is NOT
- Not aggressive. Not trying to prove anything.
- Not nostalgic. We reference the past to explain the present, not to wish we were there.
- Not preachy. We say what we do, we don't tell people what they should do.
- Not ironic. Every sentence is sincere. If you can read it as a wink, rewrite it.

### Tone by Channel
| Channel | Tone |
|---|---|
| Email / Newsletter | Founder-direct. First person. "Brooks here." |
| Product page | Cold facts first, then one line of context. |
| Instagram caption | Visual-led, minimal words, let the image carry. |
| X / Twitter | Sharp, quotable, under 140 chars when possible. |
| YouTube | Documentary-style voiceover. No hype edits. |
| Press response | Direct, generous with facts, no corporate polish. |

The hoodie itself is a premium heavyweight zip-up on an Independent Trading Co. base. Black or white. S through 2XL. $75.

I owe it to the writers who hit those walls knowing the city was going to paint over their work by Monday. I owe it to the version of me who was standing on the edge of that scene wondering if I'd ever be able to make my own contribution.

This hoodie is that contribution. That's not marketing language. That's the actual reason it exists.
